About Us

Experience the enchanting melodies of Ceylon Quartet

The Ceylon Quartet is a group of four young musicians who are passionate about bringing the beauty of string music back to life in Sri Lanka and with a shared vision of shaping a brighter future through the power of strings. Ceylon Quartet weaves together melodies that resonate with innovation and purpose, harmonising our individual talents into a symphony of inspiration. As a String quartet, we are hoping to rekindle the lost interest in string music in the country and leave a lasting legacy for years to come.

Members

Darshana Amarasinghe

Darshana is a highly skilled musician in Sri Lanka who has made a name for himself with his exceptional abilities in playing the Violin, Viola, and Cello. He is a member of several esteemed music groups in the island, including the Symphony Orchestra of Sri Lanka, Chamber Music Society of Colombo, and National Youth Orchestra of Sri Lanka. Currently, Darshana is working as the leader of the cello section in Gustav Mahler Orchestra of Colombo. He has also made significant contributions to the Sri Lankan pop and film music industry as a recording artist and a fusion violinist with Mr. Rakitha Wikramarathne and "Naadro," one of Sri Lanka's leading percussion ensembles. Darshana is passionate about sharing his love for music and serves as a visiting lecturer at the University of Visual and Performing Arts, inspiring young aspiring musicians

Sanduni Amarakoon

Sanduni Amarakoon is an undergraduate student at the “University of the Visual and Performing Arts”, where she is pursuing her passion for instrumental music. She also completed her BA Honours degree in English in 2022. In addition to her educational achievements, Sanduni is also the concertmaster of the Gustav Mahler Society of Colombo. Her love for instrumental music shines through in her performances, where she showcases her technical skills and emotional depth. Sanduni's passion for music was instilled in her from a young age, as both of her parents are also musicians. She is most drawn to instrumental music, where the human voice is not the main focus, and she finds great joy in expressing herself through her Violin and her motive is to create a better future and a safe space for female musicians.

Ravipathi Kandage

Ravipathi Kandage is an undergraduate student currently studying at the University of Visual and Performing Arts following a Bachelor's degree in Performing Arts with a focus on Western Music, and also pursuing a Bachelor's degree in Music at the SLT Research University. In addition to his impressive academic achievements, Ravipathi has also completed theory exams in Western Music and practical cello exams at Trinity College London, as well as achieving Grade 8 on the drums through the London College of Music. His passion for music extends beyond studies, as he has also conducted research on music and presented his findings at an international research symposium.

Manoj Wijayawardana

Manoj Wijayawardana is a highly talented musician with an impressive track record in violin performance. He received his Bachelor of Performing Arts degree from the University of Visual and Performing Arts in Colombo, where he majored in violin performance. Manoj has been a member of various prestigious musical groups such as the Symphony Orchestra of Sri Lanka, Chamber Music Society of Colombo (Provisional member), Krasna Chamber Ensemble, Gustav Mahler Society of Colombo, National Unity Orchestra, and National Youth Orchestra. Apart from his classical music performances, Manoj has also showcased his skills in musical theatre. Manoj is dedicated to providing education and carrying out educational programs for young and upcoming musicians, especially school children. He has a strong passion for Sri Lankan music culture and aims to create a better future for young musicians.

Barbora Valiuk

Barbora Valiukevičiūtė is a world-renowned violinist, conductor, and producer. She has performed at prestigious music venues worldwide, including the Metropolitan Museum Concert Hall and National Philharmonic of Lithuania. Barbora is the founder of the Baltic Duet and launched the annual summer music cycle, Koncertai Burmistrui, in Merkinė. She is also a sought-after teacher, having taught at the Barenboim-Said Foundation in Palestine and The Merit School of Music in Chicago. Barbora's exceptional work has made her an ambassador for the Kaunas European Capital of Culture 2022. She studied violin in Lithuania and Italy before continuing her studies in the USA with renowned violinists. Through her guidance and support, “Ceylon Quartet” is learning how to use their music to create positive change in the world. Barbora believes that music has the power to bring people together and to inspire them to work towards a better future.
